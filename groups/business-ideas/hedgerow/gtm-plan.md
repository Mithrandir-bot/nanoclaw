# Hedgerow, Go-To-Market Plan (Florida Launch)

> Language updated for compliance (no insurance/gambling terms); see gtm-paid-ads.md for the current compliant ad creative.

**Product:** Event-based marketing promotions for small businesses. A business runs a conditional promotion (or carries event exposure), pays a small flat fee, and Hedgerow funds an automatic payout if the event triggers. Risk is laid off on Kalshi (internal-only venue, never named in customer copy); payouts via USD / ACH / USDC.

**Core validated insight (from the pricing model):** The product is *most compelling and affordable for low-probability, high-excitement promos.* The flat fee is only ~**7-14% of exposure** for **5-10%** probability events, but a punishing **55-63% of exposure** for ~**50/50** events. **The wedge is fun, long-odds conditional promotions, not coin-flips.** Every targeting, creative, and sales decision below flows from this.

## 2) The Single Best Beachhead: Sports Bars & Restaurants (game-day long-odds promos)

**Pick: Florida sports bars and casual restaurants running game-day promotions.**

Why this wins as the wedge:

1. **Perfect product-pricing fit.** Long-odds in-game props (5+ TDs, a grand slam, a shutout, a hat trick, overtime) sit squarely in the 5-10% probability band, so the flat fee is only **7-14% of exposure**. The math is genuinely affordable, so the offer closes.
2. **Layoff-able.** Sports outcomes are the deepest, most liquid Kalshi markets (internal-only venue, never named in customer copy), easy to lay off risk, tight pricing.
3. **Highest frequency = fastest learning + LTV.** A sports bar can run a promo *every week* of the NFL/NBA/MLB/NHL/college season. One closed account = dozens of repeat flat fees per year. This is the only segment with weekly cadence, which compounds CAC payback.
4. **Cheapest to reach & validate.** Restaurants & bars have the lowest paid-acquisition costs of our targets (Google CPL ~$30; Meta CPL ~$18-28; restaurant CPCs $0.40-0.90). Dense, list-able, and clustered (Tampa, Miami, Orlando, Jacksonville) for founder-led walk-ins.
5. **Built-in viral demand engine.** The *end customer* (the fan) loves the promo regardless of outcome, it drives foot traffic and social buzz for the bar. The bar's own marketing does Hedgerow's customer education for free.

**Geographic micro-beachhead:** Start in **Tampa Bay**, dense sports-bar scene, passionate Bucs/Lightning/Rays fanbases, manageable size for founder-led sales, and a clear seasonal calendar to anchor weekly promos.

## 3) Channels Ranked by Cost-Efficiency (with realistic CAC)

CAC = fully-loaded cost to acquire one *paying business account*. Benchmarks below are from 2025/26 SMB ad data (sources at end). Hedgerow is a novel category, so expect the high end early until proof points exist.

| Rank | Channel | Realistic CAC (per paying SMB) | Why / Notes |
|------|---------|-------------------------------|-------------|
| **1** | **Founder-led sales (walk-ins + warm intros)** | **$50-250** (mostly time) | Highest conversion for a new, trust-dependent category. Walk into 15-20 Tampa sports bars before a big game weekend, demo a live promo. Essential for the first 20-30 logos and for learning objections. Doesn't scale, but cheapest per dollar early. |
| **2** | **Partnerships, POS vendors (Toast, Square, Clover), restaurant associations, local marketing agencies** | **$100-400** | Highest-leverage scalable channel. POS integrations put Hedgerow in front of exactly the right merchants; agencies resell it as a "promo product." Florida Restaurant & Lodging Association (FRLA) and local chambers give credibility + lists. Longer to land but compounding. |
| **3** | **Meta (Facebook/Instagram), local biz targeting** | **$150-500** (CPL ~$18-28, then sales conversion) | Best paid channel for this segment: restaurant CPMs $7-12, CPCs $0.40-0.90, Meta CPL ~60% cheaper than Google. Target bar/restaurant owners + lookalikes. Great for the *waitlist* funnel. |
| **4** | **Google Search (high-intent capture)** | **$200-600** (Restaurants CPL ~$30, B2B intent higher) | Low search volume for a category nobody Googles yet ("event-based promo," "weather refund promo"). Use for branded + a few high-intent terms; not a primary acquisition engine pre-category-awareness. |
| **5** | **Content / SEO** | **$50-150 long-run, ~$0 short-run conversions** | Slow burn. "How to run a game-day promo that fills your bar" + free promo-odds calculator. Builds the category and feeds organic over 6-12 months. Not for the $1k test. |
| **6** | **Cold email / cold outreach** | **$300-800** | Cold converts far worse than referrals (~26% conversion) for trust-heavy financial products. Useful for booking founder demos at scale, but low standalone close rate. Secondary. |

**Headline:** founder-led + partnerships are the durable engine. Meta is the cheapest *paid* test channel for demand validation.

## 4) Concrete $1,000 Test-Budget Plan (Validate Demand)

**Goal:** Prove SMBs *want* this and will *intend to pay*, before building checkout. Validate with a waitlist + quote-completion funnel and founder demos. No real money changes hands yet; capture intent (signed LOI / "reserve my promo" + card-on-file optional).

### Channel mix & exact allocation ($1,000)

| Allocation | Spend | Use |
|-----------|-------|-----|
| **Meta Ads (FL sports-bar/restaurant owner targeting)** | **$500** | Primary paid demand test. 2 campaigns: (A) Owner lead-gen to waitlist; (B) Retargeting of landing-page visitors. Target: FL, interests = restaurant owners / bar owners / Toast / Square / FRLA + lookalike of any seed list. |
| **Google Search (branded + 5 high-intent terms)** | **$150** | Capture the few who actively search ("sports promo marketing," "weather refund promo," "free if my team wins promo"). Tight exact-match, low daily cap. |
| **Landing page + funnel tooling** | **$100** | Carrd/Webflow/Framer + a Typeform-style quote estimator + Calendly + email (the "quote completion" step). |
| **Promo-odds quote calculator / creative + a small incentive** | **$150** | Simple interactive "What would this promo cost me?" tool (enter exposure + pick a long-odds event, shows flat fee = 7-14%). Plus $X Amazon/Uber Eats card incentive to book a founder demo. |
| **Reserve / founder-led walk-ins (gas, printed one-pagers, samples)** | **$100** | Founder visits ~20 Tampa Bay sports bars before a marquee game weekend. Zero-CAC logos + qualitative gold. |

### Targeting

- **Geo:** Tampa Bay metro (then Orlando if budget allows).
- **Audience:** Owners/GMs of sports bars and casual restaurants; interest + behavior targeting (Toast/Square users, FRLA, "sports bar," "game day").
- **Seasonality:** Time the test to a high-attention sports window (e.g., NBA/NHL playoffs, an MLB stretch, or pre-NFL-season hype) so promos feel timely.

### Creative / offer angle

- **Hook:** *"Run a promo your fans will lose their minds over, and only pay if it actually happens."*
- **Concrete example in the ad:** *"'Free wings if the Bucs score 5 touchdowns.' Costs you ~$10 for a $100 payout. We pay your customers automatically if it hits."*
- **CTA:** "Get my free promo quote", quote calculator, "Reserve my promo / Join the launch list."

### Landing flow (waitlist now, checkout later)

1. Ad, then **landing page** (the offer + 1 example + a "payouts funded and automatic" trust line).
2. **Quote calculator** (exposure + event, instant flat-fee %). This is the key intent signal.
3. **"Reserve my promo"** form, email + phone + desired event/date (+ optional card-on-file for "notify me when live").
4. Auto-email + **Calendly** for a founder demo. Founder closes a verbal/LOI commitment.

### Success metrics (decision gates)

| Metric | Target (go signal) |
|--------|--------------------|
| Landing to quote-calculator start | ≥ 30% of visitors |
| Quote completions | ≥ 50 in the test window |
| Waitlist signups (intent) | ≥ 75 total (paid + walk-in) |
| Demo bookings | ≥ 15 |
| **Hard-intent commitments** (LOI / "reserve" / card-on-file) | **≥ 8-10**, *the real validation* |
| Blended cost per qualified intent | ≤ $50 |
| Walk-in close-to-commit rate | ≥ 25% |

**Kill / pivot signal:** < 20 quote completions or < 3 hard commitments means the offer or segment is wrong; re-test creative or move to car-dealership long-odds promos.

### 3-4 Week Timeline

- **Week 1, Build & seed.** Stand up landing page + quote calculator + Calendly + tracking. Draft 4-6 Meta creatives + Google keywords. Build the 20-bar Tampa walk-in list. Launch ads end of week ($50/day Meta, $15/day Google).
- **Week 2, Run & walk.** Ads live. Founder does first 10 walk-ins around a game weekend. Daily creative pruning; kill losers, scale winners. Start booking demos.
- **Week 3, Convert & deepen.** Remaining 10 walk-ins. Run founder demos from inbound. Push for hard-intent commitments / LOIs. Retargeting campaign on.
- **Week 4, Read & decide.** Compile metrics vs. gates. Decide: scale beachhead, expand to Orlando, or pivot segment. Document objections + winning offer language for the real-checkout build.

## 5) Messaging / Positioning

### To the business (the buyer)

**Positioning statement:** *Hedgerow lets you run bold, viral promotions without risking the register. Run the promo your customers can't stop talking about, pay a small flat fee, and if it hits, we fund the payout, automatically.*

Core messages, in priority order:
1. **Risk-free hype.** "Run a promo wild enough to go viral, capped, known cost. No surprise blowout to your P&L."
2. **It's cheap because it's long-odds.** "Fund $1,000 in payouts for ~$70-140. The crazier (less likely) the promo, the cheaper it is."
3. **Automatic & instant.** "If it triggers, your customers get paid automatically, USD, ACH, or USDC. You don't lift a finger or cut checks."
4. **Drives traffic now.** "The promo fills your bar whether or not it hits. The buzz is the point."
5. **Trust / credibility.** "We fund the payout from real markets, not a gimmick. Known payout, paid automatically."

Tone: punchy, confident, operator-to-operator. Lead with a concrete dollar example, never with the word "insurance" (sounds slow, expensive, regulated). Frame as a **marketing product.**

### To the end customer (the promo audience / fans)

Hedgerow gives the business turnkey copy to put on table tents, social, and signage:
- **"Free wings if the Bucs score 5 TDs tonight. We mean it, payout's automatic."**
- **"Drinks on the house if there's a grand slam. Stick around and find out."**
- **"Snow in Miami on Christmas? Your car's free."** (dealership variant)

End-customer angle: **excitement + a real reward that pays automatically.** The hook is the long-odds thrill ("imagine if it actually happens") plus the credibility that *it will actually pay*, turning a marketing promotion into a genuine shared moment the crowd roots for together. That communal "are we gonna win?!" energy is the foot-traffic and social engine that makes the business buy again.
